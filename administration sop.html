SOP: Administration — Patient Records & Admissions

Purpose

This SOP defines the standardized process for admitting patients, maintaining medical records, ensuring data accuracy, and protecting patient privacy in compliance with health information regulations.

Scope

Applicable to all administrative staff involved in patient registration, admissions, billing, and medical record handling.

Responsibilities

  • Admissions Clerk: Collect and verify patient personal details and medical insurance information.
  • Records Officer: Create, update, and archive patient files in both electronic and paper formats.
  • Billing Department: Manage patient billing, discharge forms, and financial clearances.
  • Privacy Officer: Ensure confidentiality and compliance with data protection laws (POPIA/HIPAA).

Procedures

  1. Admission: Confirm patient identity, register in the EMR system, and assign a Medical Record Number (MRN).
  2. Record Management: Scan paper forms and upload them to the EMR system within 2 hours of patient registration.
  3. Confidentiality: Restrict access to authorized personnel; maintain an audit trail of record access.
  4. Discharge: Verify discharge summaries, update records, and finalize billing within 24 hours post-discharge.
